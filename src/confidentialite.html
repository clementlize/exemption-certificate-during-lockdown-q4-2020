Trackers and cookies

During the consultation of the generator, cookies are deposited on the terminal used. These cookies are used to to secure the proposed service.

Definition of a cookie

A "cookie" is a string of information, usually small amounts of data, identified by a name, which can be transmitted to your browser by a website you connect to. Your web browser keeps it for a certain period of time, and refers it back to the web server each time you go there. Cookies have multiple uses: they can be used to memorize your customer ID, the current contents of your shopping cart, an identifier allowing you to trace your navigation for statistical or advertising purposes, etc.

Means of opposing the deposit of cookies via your browser

If you are using Firefox : Click on the menu button and select "Options". Next, select the "Privacy and Security" panel. Go to the "Enhanced Tracking Protection" section. Choose custom protection [radio button "Custom"]. Uncheck the "cookies" box. Click on the button "Refresh all tabs" if you want to apply these changes to the current navigation within your browser.

If you are using Chrome : Top right, click on "More > Settings". At the bottom, click on "Advanced Settings". In "Privacy and security", click on "Site Settings". Click on "Cookies and site data". Disable the option "Allow sites to store/read cookie data".

If you are using Internet Explorer : Select the "Tools" button, then "Internet Options". Select the "Privacy" tab and then under "Settings". If you want to use the "Advanced" option, select "Advanced". Check the box "Ignore management cookies automatically", then select "Deny" in the "Third party cookies" column.

For more information on this topic, you can browse the CNIL website (french).
